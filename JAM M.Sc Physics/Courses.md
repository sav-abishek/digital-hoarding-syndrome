Self study is the best way to prepare for JAM. (Assuming you are from physics background)

If you have a good internet connection then you can download them for free!

Here are list of some web sites:

MIT OCW Course 8.01, 8.02, 80.3 By Walter Lewin For preliminary physics

Special relativity from NPTEL By Shiva Prasad (IIT Bombay)

MIT OCW course 8.04 by Allan Adams , 8.05 by Barton Zwiebach for Quantum Mechanics

NPTEL Classical and quantum physics by Prof. Balakrishnan

MIT OCW course 5.60 by Moungi Bawendi for Thermodynamics and kinematics

NCERT 10,11 and 12th standard text books which are available online on official website

That’s almost all for JAM if not more.There’s a book named “IIT-Jam Physics” by R.Gupta it contains all 26 years previous exam papers with solution.
